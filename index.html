About me

My journey in engineering is driven by a simple philosophy: understand the first principles, learn relentlessly, and execute under pressure.

I have hands-on experience building across the ML spectrum—from classical regression and ensemble methods (Decision Trees, Random Forests) to PCA and Deep Neural Networks.

However, technology is only as valuable as the problems it solves. Driven by a deeply personal connection, my flagship research focuses on developing a predictive model for Japanese Encephalitis. For me, engineering is not just an academic pursuit; it is a tool to create tangible, life-saving impact.
